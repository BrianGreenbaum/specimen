By the close of the 18th century the serif had tipped from reason into spectacle. **Didot** in Paris and **Bodoni** in Parma pushed the contrast between thick and thin to a glamorous extreme — heavy stems, vanishing hairlines, a strict vertical axis — and invented the look of luxury that fashion mastheads still wear today.

DM Serif Display, drawn by the **Colophon Foundry** for Google's open DM family, brings that drama to the screen with one crucial softening: where a pure Didone snaps its serifs on flat, DM Serif keeps a gentle **bracket** and an old-style warmth. The result is high-contrast and unmistakably editorial, but a little more approachable — a headline serif that flatters a cover line without the brittleness of true Bodoni at smaller display sizes.

It comes in a single weight with a true italic, and it is built for exactly one job, which it does beautifully: the large line. Set a title in DM Serif Display and the hairlines bloom, the stems anchor, and the words take on the confident glamour of a magazine masthead. In the timeline it is the Didone spirit, drawn for the headline of the digital page.
