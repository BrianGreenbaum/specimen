**Unbounded** arrived in 2022 from designer Roman Shchupak as a typeface that wears its ambition in its name. Where most geometric sans-serifs nod toward the rational grids of the Bauhaus, Unbounded takes a different approach: every corner is rounded, every terminal softened, every junction given a curve instead of a cut. The result is a display family that feels simultaneously machine-precise and humanly approachable — geometric in skeleton, but warm in finish.

The family spans eight weights from ExtraLight 200 to Black 900, all delivered as a single variable font file on the wght axis. That range is unusually complete for a display face: the lighter instances read as refined and editorial while the heavier weights compress into powerful, logo-ready lockups where rounded counters shrink to bold slivers. The variable axis means a web designer can drive a headline from thin to black in a single animation, exploiting a quality that distinguishes the face from any static release.

Unbounded's rounded geometry places it in a specific moment in type history — a wave of screen-first display faces designed in the early 2020s that rejected both the cold precision of mid-century geometric grotesques and the nostalgic curves of 1970s round-letter revivals. This is not Avant Garde redone; the proportions are wider, the counters more aggressively circular, the stroke weight more even at heavy settings than any optically-corrected predecessor would allow. It is a face that was drawn on screen and was always intended to live there.

The face has found a natural home in branding and digital product work: wordmarks, app dashboards, streaming platforms, game UIs, and festival identities. Its Cyrillic support extends this reach beyond Latin-only design systems. Open-source under the SIL Open Font License, Unbounded sits on Google Fonts and has become a reliable tool for any brand that wants a display voice that is bold, rounded, and unmistakably contemporary.
