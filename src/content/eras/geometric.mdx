In the 1920s a generation of modernists decided the alphabet should be redesigned from first principles. The hand was a relic; the future would be **constructed**. At the Bauhaus and in the orbit of *Die Neue Typographie*, the new letter was assembled from the elementary shapes of geometry — the circle, the triangle, the straight line — as rationally as a building.

The masterpiece was **Futura**, drawn by **Paul Renner** in 1927: an o that is almost a perfect circle, an a stripped to a single storey, strokes of near-perfect **monoline** evenness. It rejected the **grotesque’s** drawn-by-eye irregularity in favour of pure design, on a drafting table, from ideas. Crisp, optimistic and unmistakably *modern*, the geometric sans became the voice of the twentieth century — and the direct ancestor of a hundred years of clean, confident brand type.
